Introduction

The purpose of this playbook is to provide a project based framework that outlines a consistent and effective process for HR activity in business reorganizations, for HR teams to leverage in planning and executing org design projects.

The Playbook will support global HR professionals:

  • To explain HR responsibilities in a business reorganizations
  • To have clarity for effective collaboration between HR teams for efficient delivery
  • To define scope of each HR domains
  • To identify the Organization Design process and phases
  • To list the tool kit available for Organization Design
  • To allow knowledge sharing

Together with the Org Design Toolkit the playbook describes a collaborative HR effort to more effectively initiate and execute org design projects in Cargill.

This Playbook is not intended to be a detailed project plan or to include tools required by GHRS domains to execute work activities. Every reorganization has its own specifics, however the core deliverables per service area are provided to assist in building a project plan.

The playbook aligns the Cargill Project Delivery Process PDP with the Talent Performance Org Design Phasing; outlining the core steps to be taken at each phase, the roles and responsibilities of the team members and references the tools to use.

The Appendix refers to continuous improvement and feedback mechanisms that will allow the BRM team to maintain this playbook as a relevant guide for HR and a list of typical tools and templates with reference to phase and where to locate them.

What is Org Design?

Organization Design is a proactive fact-based approach to analyze an organizational structure in terms of process, governance (decision rights), people effort, costs and operating model efficiency and effectiveness. This process ensures the business has the right capabilities and capacity to drive strategic success.

Org Design is the right solution when an organization needs

  • To realign their people to a new strategy, market change or adapt due to competitive pressure
  • To integrate or separate organizations due to MADJV
  • To optimize a current structure to be more efficient and effective

Structure is only one element of an integrated system, the other dimensions being strategy, process, people and culture. The Organization Design methodology considers the interdependence with these other system dimensions, and follows the Project Delivery Process (PDP) stage gate model.

The Global Org Design Toolkit provides a consistent, end-to-end process based org design methodology and toolkit for org design efforts in Cargill. It will assist in building capability in HR and business leaders to leverage strategic and operational org design as a business management best practice to support our growth and operational discipline goals.

Strategic Org Design refers to top down strategy based org design (clean sheet, strategic, building new capabilities) and includes redefining operating models and leadership team governance and structures.

Operational Org Design refers to bottoms up evaluation of an organization, focusing on optimizing layers and simplifying structures or possibly consolidating work across teams in order to find efficiencies and greater effectiveness.
